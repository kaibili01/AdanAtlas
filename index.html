Work

Selected projects showcasing product design, policy frameworks, and community-driven outcomes.

View Project
California PRTH Housing Authority

Website Redesign & Community Housing Strategy. This project involved a full redesign of the Pit River Tribal Housing Authority’s digital presence. The goal was to create an accessible, culturally respectful, and modern interface that communicates housing programs, eligibility resources, and community services. The design prioritizes clarity, mobile responsiveness, and future scalability — all while integrating tribal visual language and honoring the Pit River Nation's identity.

View Project
ESSA Tribal Consultation Presentation

A strategic presentation designed for the Nevada Department of Education to guide effective ESSA Tribal Consultation practices. The slides detail consultation obligations, timelines, and culturally responsive approaches for engaging with Tribal Nations in the planning and implementation of federally funded education programs.

View Project
Phenomenal Type Cultivars

A visual identity and product positioning deck for a culturally grounded cannabis cultivar based in Coachella, California. Positioned to leverage the city's globally recognized music festivals, the project emphasizes community reinvestment and economic equity. It seeks to create employment opportunities for individuals—disproportionately people of color—who were impacted by cannabis prohibition but have been left out of legalization's benefits.

View Project
Farmshare

A concept presentation for a mobile application designed to connect homeowners with surplus fruit trees to individuals seeking fresh, locally grown produce. FarmShare promotes a mutual exchange where both parties benefit—homeowners reduce food waste while harvesters gain access to nutritious food. The platform supports community resilience, environmental sustainability, and equitable food distribution through accessible, tech-enabled sharing.

View Project
Grupo Huellas

This cross-border initiative united stakeholders from both sides of the Tijuana border to address the significant environmental challenge posed by discarded automobile tires. The project focused on raising awareness about the harmful impacts these tires have on the Tijuana Estuary ecosystem—a critical habitat that directly affects the San Diego region as well. By fostering binational collaboration, Grupo Huellas aimed to promote sustainable solutions to mitigate pollution and protect this shared environmental resource.

View Project
Facebook Orange

This project explored the concept of wellness in the workplace through the lens of social networks. Focusing on how platforms like Facebook foster communication, community, and networking, the initiative sought to understand how digital environments can support employee well-being. By examining the intersection of technology and workplace culture, Facebook Orange uncovered new insights into how social connectivity contributes to a healthier, more engaged workforce.
